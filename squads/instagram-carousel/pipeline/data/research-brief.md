# Research Brief: Ophthalmology Instagram Carousel Content
**Client:** Dr. Thiago Russo — CEDOA, Manaus, Brazil
**Audience:** General public interested in eye health
**Format Focus:** Instagram carousels for patient education
**Date:** 2026-03-18

---

## Key Findings

1. **Ophthalmology is Instagram-native as a specialty** — it is inherently visual, making it exceptionally well-suited to image and carousel formats. The American Academy of Ophthalmology (AAO) has over 30,000 followers and is among the leading professional organizations on the platform. [Source: PMC8921826 — Confidence: HIGH]

2. **Most eye health content on Instagram is created by non-ophthalmologists**, and educational content has the lowest organic engagement. This creates a blue-ocean opportunity: ophthalmologists who blend education with personality systematically outperform faceless institutional accounts. [Source: PMC8921826 — Confidence: HIGH]

3. **Carousels outperform all other formats in engagement quality** in 2026. Average carousel engagement rate: 10% vs. single images 7% vs. Reels 6%. Carousels also generate 3.1× more engagement than standard posts and benefit from Instagram's re-serve mechanic (unseen slides are reshown). [Source: TrueFuture Media, SocialInsider — Confidence: HIGH]

4. **Saves and DM shares are the two most powerful algorithmic signals** in 2026, per Instagram head Adam Mosseri. Carousels generate more saves than any other format. Educational, checklist-style content drives saves. Relatable, shareable content drives DM sends — both are essential for reaching non-followers. [Source: Buffer, Sprout Social, Hootsuite — Confidence: HIGH]

5. **CFM Resolução 2.336/2023 is in effect since March 2024**, significantly liberalizing what Brazilian doctors can post on Instagram while retaining strict anti-sensationalism and anti-guaranty rules. Carousels are explicitly viable under the "educational content" clause. [Source: CFM Portal, CREMERS — Confidence: HIGH]

6. **The highest-performing ophthalmology topics on Instagram** are glaucoma (100% of posts rated educational), cataract surgery (patient curiosity is extremely high), dry eye (extremely relatable — linked to screen time), and vision-threatening diseases awareness. [Source: PMC8921826 — Confidence: HIGH]

7. **First-slide hooks drive everything.** Meta's internal data shows carousels where 70%+ of slides are viewed are distributed to 3–5× more non-followers. The hook slide has ~3 seconds to earn the swipe. [Source: Marketing Agent Blog — Confidence: MEDIUM-HIGH]

8. **AI-generated ophthalmology content is rising rapidly in 2026** and raising trust concerns. Physician-authored, verifiable content from a real named doctor is increasingly valuable as a differentiator. [Source: Nature Eye journal 2026 — Confidence: HIGH]

9. **Brazilians are a highly engaged social media audience**, and ophthalmology clinics that post consistent, accessible-language content in Portuguese build patient bases directly from Instagram. Location-specific hashtags increase discoverability within Manaus. [Source: Eyecare Blog, Universo Visual — Confidence: MEDIUM]

10. **Short-form video (Reels) is for discovery; carousels are for depth and retention.** The optimal 2026 mix is 60–70% Reels for reach + 20–30% carousels for education and saves. A squad producing carousels should treat them as high-value, save-optimized assets, not just content filler. [Source: TrueFuture Media 2026 — Confidence: HIGH]

11. **Keyword-rich captions now outperform hashtag stacking** for discovery. Instagram limits posts to 5 effective hashtags. SEO within captions (e.g., "glaucoma Manaus", "cirurgia de catarata") is the emerging discovery mechanism. [Source: Sprout Social 2026 — Confidence: HIGH]

12. **Personalidade + credibilidade = viral.** The most-engaged ophthalmology posts feature personal experiences, physicians in white coats, and trainee perspectives. Pure informational posts without personality score the lowest engagement. [Source: PMC8921826 — Confidence: HIGH]

## CFM Compliance Rules

**Governing document:** Resolução CFM nº 2.336/2023 — effective March 11, 2024
**Enforcement body:** CODAME (Comissão de Divulgação de Assuntos Médicos) at regional CFM councils

### What Is PERMITTED for Dr. Thiago Russo's Instagram

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Educational carousels | Fully allowed. Content must have genuine educational character. Carousels explaining conditions, procedures, and prevention are compliant. |
| Before/after images | Allowed with strict conditions (see below). |
| Patient images | Allowed with prior written consent, guaranteed anonymity (no identification even with consent), and educational framing. |
| Patient testimonials | Allowed. Can be reposted. Cannot contain superlatives guaranteeing results. |
| Clinic equipment & technology | Allowed if ANVISA-approved and CFM-recognized. No claim of superiority. |
| Selfies / team photos | Allowed. No sensationalism. |
| Consultation pricing | Allowed (consultation fees only — not procedure pricing). |
| Promotions / discounts | Allowed as long as no bundled sales, prizes, or competition-distorting offers. |
| Giveaways | PROHIBITED. No sorteios (prize draws) of any kind. |
| Interviews / educational videos | Fully allowed. Must be informative, not self-promotional in tone. |
| Partnerships with influencers | PROHIBITED under current CFM code. |

### Before/After Image Rules (Critical)

Before/after content — which is high-performing carousel content — requires strict adherence:
- Must include educational text with: therapeutic indications, factors that may affect results, and description of possible complications
- Must show a set of images including satisfactory evolutions, unsatisfactory evolutions, and complications (not just good outcomes)
- Where possible: show results across different biotypes and age groups
- Show short-term, medium-term, and long-term evolutions
- Patient must NOT be identified — even if they consent
- Images must not be manipulated, filtered, or artificially enhanced
- Use at minimum 4 different patients to represent the range of outcomes

### Mandatory Profile Identification

Every professional Instagram profile must clearly display:
- Full name
- CRM number (including state — e.g., CRM/AM XXXXX)
- Medical specialty (maximum 2 specialties)
- RQE (Registro de Qualificação de Especialista) number if applicable
- Address, phone, and email

### Content Reposting Responsibility

Any patient or third-party post that Dr. Russo reposts is treated as his own publication for compliance purposes. He is responsible for all reposts complying with CFM rules.

### Key Anti-Sensationalism Rule

All content must avoid:
- Claims of being the "best," "most advanced," or "only" provider
- Language guaranteeing results
- Emotional manipulation or fear-mongering to attract patients
- Teaching medical techniques to non-physicians

## Carousel Optimization Insights

### Format Performance Data (2026)

- Average carousel engagement rate: **10.15%** (highest of all formats)
- Carousels achieve **1.4× wider reach** than single images
- **3.1× higher engagement** than standard posts
- Carousels with 70%+ swipe-through rate are distributed to **3–5× more non-followers**
- Instagram re-serves carousels to users who didn't finish them — providing multiple impression windows

### Optimal Structure

| Element | Best Practice |
|---|---|
| Slide count | 8–12 for educational posts; up to 20 for deep guides |
| First slide | Bold hook — earns the swipe in under 3 seconds |
| Slide 2 | Deliver on the hook promise immediately |
| Mid-carousel | Soft CTA ("save this for later") |
| Last slide | Hard CTA (DM, follow, book appointment) |
| Text per slide | Maximum 20% text coverage; 1 idea per slide; readable in 1–2 seconds |
| Visual design | Consistent brand colors, high contrast, no busy layouts |

### Hook Patterns That Work (Apply These to Eye Health Content)

1. **Curiosity Gap:** "The silent eye thief that has no symptoms — swipe to see if you're at risk" (glaucoma)
2. **Mistake/Fear:** "5 things you're doing right now that are damaging your eyes" (screen habits)
3. **Contrarian/Bold Statement:** "Reading glasses at 45 are not inevitable — swipe to find out why"
4. **Transformation/Before-After:** Simulated vision with vs. without cataracts — "This is how the world looks with cataracts. Swipe."
5. **Numbered List Promise:** "7 signs your child might need glasses (most parents miss #4)"
6. **Open Loop:** "Your ophthalmologist is checking for this at every appointment — do you know what it is?"

### Posting Frequency

- **Carousels:** 1–2 per week (high-effort, high-reward format)
- **Reels:** 2–3 per week (for discovery and reach)
- **Stories:** Daily or near-daily (relationship maintenance with followers)
- Total target: **4–7 posts/week** across formats

### Algorithmic Signals to Optimize For (Priority Order)

1. **DM Shares** — design content people want to send to family members ("Send this to someone who stares at screens all day")
2. **Saves** — design content people want to reference later (checklists, symptom guides, care tips)
3. **Swipe depth** — structure carousels so each slide pulls the reader forward
4. **Likes per reach** — secondary; don't optimize for likes at the expense of saves/shares

### SEO in Captions (2026 Priority)

Use natural-language keywords in the caption body:
- "oftalmologista em Manaus"
- "cirurgia de catarata Manaus"
- "glaucoma sintomas"
- "olho seco tratamento"
- "saúde ocular"

Hashtags: limit to 5, use specific ones: `#oftalmologista`, `#saudeocular`, `#glaucoma`, `#cedoa`, `#manaus`

## Medical Content Patterns

### What Makes Medical Content Perform

| Pattern | Mechanism | Application for Dr. Russo |
|---|---|---|
| **Physician personality** | Builds trust and parasocial connection | Show Dr. Russo explaining conditions to camera, not just text-only carousels |
| **White coat authority** | Highest engagement signal in ophthalmology research | Include Dr. Russo in clinical setting on cover slides |
| **Myth-busting** | High share rate — people send corrections to others | "Carrots don't actually cure bad eyesight — here's what really helps" |
| **Relatable everyday problems** | High organic save rate | Screen fatigue, dry eyes, blurry vision — things people experience daily |
| **Behind-the-scenes OR/clinic** | Humanizes the practice, builds trust | Equipment showcases, pre/post-op walkthroughs (with compliance rules) |
| **Question-and-answer format** | Positions doctor as accessible expert | "You asked, I answered: the most common eye health questions" |
| **Condition explainers** | Highly shareable — patients share to family | "What is glaucoma?" / "What happens during cataract surgery?" |
| **Awareness month content** | Amplified by community hashtags | January (Glaucoma), June (Cataract Awareness), October (World Sight Day) |
| **Fear/urgency content** | Highest save rate | "5 warning signs you should see an ophthalmologist THIS WEEK" |

### Top Medical Influencer Success Patterns

- **Dr. Karan Rajan (NHS):** Debunks health myths + gives practical tips → 1.4M+ Instagram followers
- **Dr. Sandra Lee:** Visual specialty (dermatology) + procedure videos → highest-paid medical influencer
- **"Dancing Doctor":** Personality + relatability + humanizing healthcare → viral reach

The pattern for ophthalmology: **procedure visibility + educational depth + accessible language + physician personality** = sustainable viral potential.

### Language Strategy for Brazilian Audience

- Replace all jargon with plain-language equivalents
- Instead of "ceratocone em estágio avançado com ectasia" → "quando a córnea está muito fina e torta, dificultando a visão mesmo com óculos"
- Instead of "pressão intraocular elevada" → "pressão dentro do olho que pode machucar o nervo óptico"
- Target reading level: grade 7–8 equivalent
- Use first-person and conversational tone in captions

## Recommendations

### 1. Build a "Save-First" Carousel Architecture
Every carousel produced for Dr. Russo's account should be engineered to be saved, not just liked. This means ending each carousel with a practical reference item — a checklist, a symptom guide, or a decision rule (e.g., "When to see an ophthalmologist urgently"). Saves are the top algorithmic signal for carousels and directly drive future reach to non-followers.

### 2. Prioritize High-Share Hooks Around Everyday Problems
The content angles most likely to generate DM shares (the #1 non-follower distribution signal) are topics that affect people's daily lives: screen fatigue and dry eye, myopia in children, and reading glasses in middle age. Lead each week with one of these high-relatability topics. The caption CTA should explicitly invite sharing: "Manda esse carrossel para alguém que passa o dia na frente do computador."

### 3. Establish a "Dr. Russo" Visual Identity on the Hook Slide
Research consistently shows that posts featuring the physician in a white coat achieve the highest engagement in ophthalmology. The cover slide of every carousel should include Dr. Russo's image or a recognizable visual identifier (clinic setting, CEDOA branding). This builds the parasocial trust that drives follows and appointment bookings.

### 4. Implement Full CFM Compliance From Day One
Before/after content, patient images, and testimonials are high-performing formats that are now compliant — but require strict adherence to Resolução 2.336/2023. The squad should include a compliance checklist step before any carousel goes live. Specifically: no patient identification, no result guarantees, no superlatives, mandatory profile CRM/RQE identification, and educational framing for all before/after sequences.

### 5. Create a Recurring Series Structure for Each Awareness Month
Anchor the content calendar around ophthalmology awareness months: January (Glaucoma), June (Cataract Awareness Month), October (World Sight Day — October 10). Each month should have a dedicated carousel mini-series (3–4 carousels over the month). This creates predictable, save-worthy content with natural community amplification via awareness hashtags. In Brazil, add "Dia do Oftalmologista" (January 11) as a local anchor event for engagement content.

## Gaps

The following areas could not be adequately researched or found limited specific data:

1. **CEDOA clinic's current Instagram presence and baseline metrics** — no public data found. Benchmarking against competitors in Manaus would require direct account access or social listening tools.

2. **Manaus-specific audience demographics** — while general Brazilian Instagram behavior patterns are well-documented, Manaus as a regional market (Amazônia, lower digital penetration in some demographics, strong regional identity) lacks dedicated social media research. Content tone and dialect calibration may need adjustment via audience testing.

3. **Competitor analysis for ophthalmology in Manaus/North Brazil** — no publicly available breakdown of which local ophthalmology accounts are performing well or what content topics are already saturated in the regional market.

4. **CFM enforcement precedents (CODAME cases)** — while the rules of Resolução 2.336/2023 are clear, there is limited public data on which types of content have actually been sanctioned. The compliance boundaries in practice (vs. theory) remain somewhat opaque.

5. **Portuguese-language carousel performance data** — all carousel engagement benchmarks found are primarily from US/English-language markets. Brazilian audience swipe behavior, save rates, and hook effectiveness may differ and should be validated through A/B testing after launch.

6. **Specific hashtag volume data for #oftalmologista and #saudeocular in Brazil** — general hashtag recommendations were found, but real-time volume and competition levels for Manaus-relevant tags were not available without a social listening tool (e.g., Sprout Social, Later, or native Instagram Insights).
