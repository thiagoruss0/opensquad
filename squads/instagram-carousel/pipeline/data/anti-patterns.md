# Anti-Patterns: Ophthalmology Instagram Carousel

## Medical Content Anti-Patterns

### Never Do

1. **Guarantee results or cure rates**: "99% de sucesso na cirurgia de catarata" violates CFM rules and misleads patients. Instead: "A cirurgia de catarata tem altas taxas de satisfação, mas resultados variam conforme cada caso."

2. **Use medical jargon without translation**: "Degeneração macular relacionada à idade com drusas confluentes" means nothing to patients. Always provide plain-language equivalent alongside or instead of technical terms.

3. **Show before/after with only successful outcomes**: CFM Resolução 2.336/2023 requires showing satisfactory AND unsatisfactory outcomes, using 4+ different patients. Cherry-picking only the best results is both non-compliant and unethical.

4. **Fear-monger without actionable advice**: "Você pode ficar cego!" without follow-up steps is irresponsible. Always pair urgency with specific next steps the patient can take.

5. **Copy competitor content without verification**: Reposted medical claims from other accounts may be inaccurate. Every medical statement must be independently verified regardless of source.

6. **Use AI-generated medical images**: Synthetic eye images, fake fundoscopy photos, or AI-generated anatomical illustrations can contain inaccuracies. Use real clinical images (with compliance) or verified medical illustrations.

7. **Promote off-label treatments or unproven therapies**: Only discuss ANVISA-approved and CFM-recognized treatments and technologies.

## Instagram Content Anti-Patterns

### Never Do

1. **Links in captions**: Instagram captions don't support clickable links. Including URLs wastes characters. Use "link na bio" or Stories link sticker instead.

2. **Hashtag spam**: More than 5 effective hashtags triggers algorithmic suppression. Never use 20-30 hashtags. 5 targeted hashtags outperform 30 generic ones.

3. **Generic stock photos**: Overly polished stock imagery signals "advertisement" and reduces organic engagement. Use real clinic photos, Dr. Russo's image, or custom-designed graphics.

4. **Posting without a CTA**: Posts without a clear ask get saved and shared significantly less. Every carousel must tell the audience what to do: save, share, comment, or visit.

5. **Text-only slides without visual hierarchy**: Walls of same-size text are unreadable on mobile. Always use two-layer hierarchy (large headline + smaller supporting text) with accent color highlights.

6. **Inconsistent visual identity**: Each carousel looking completely different destroys brand recognition. Use a consistent design system across all posts.

7. **Posting and ghosting**: Not responding to comments in the first hour kills algorithmic distribution. Plan for post-publication engagement.

8. **Slide number counters**: "1/8", "7/8" in carousel images. Instagram shows native navigation. Adding counters creates visual noise.

## Copywriting Anti-Patterns

### Never Do

1. **Start with "Você sabia que..." or "Neste post vou falar sobre..."**: These are overused openers that signal generic content. Use a specific, scroll-stopping hook instead.

2. **Write walls of text in captions**: On mobile, 3 lines is already a visual block. Use line breaks aggressively. One thought per paragraph.

3. **Use superlatives without proof**: "O melhor tratamento" or "A clínica mais avançada" violates CFM rules AND is bad copy. Replace with specific proof: "30 anos em Manaus, 3 unidades, mais de X procedimentos realizados."

4. **End with "Espero que tenha gostado!"**: This is not a CTA. Replace with a specific action: "Salve esse carrossel para consultar depois" or "Manda para alguém que precisa ver isso."

5. **Use em dashes**: They slow reading rhythm. Use periods, colons, or line breaks instead.

## Always Do

1. **Lead with the hook**: Write the hook before anything else. If it doesn't stop a scroll, nothing else matters.

2. **Translate every medical term**: For every technical term used, provide the plain-language equivalent in the same sentence or slide.

3. **Include a save-worthy element**: At least one slide should be worth saving as reference (checklist, symptom list, care tip).

4. **Match CTA to content type**: Educational content → "Salve". Relatable content → "Manda para alguém". Controversial → "Comenta". Service-related → "Agende sua consulta".

5. **Verify CFM compliance before publishing**: Run through the compliance checklist for every post. One violation can result in CRM sanctions.

6. **Use SEO keywords in captions**: "oftalmologista em Manaus", "cirurgia de catarata", "saúde ocular" help Instagram's search algorithm surface the content.
